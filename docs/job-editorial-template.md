# Job Editorial Template

Use this structure when a job page should be strong enough for indexing and ad review.

## Recommended shape

```md
## Editorial review

Explain what kind of candidate this role fits, what level it seems aimed at, and why the listing is worth opening. Add interpretation, not just a rewrite of the job description.

## Why this opening may be worth a closer look

- Mention the strongest role signals from the source.
- Call out any practical requirement that could block weak-fit applicants.
- Mention location, work mode, shift pattern, or salary uncertainty when relevant.

## Resume angle for this role

Explain how a candidate should position projects, tools, coursework, experience, or outcomes for this specific role.

tip: Add one concrete improvement a candidate can make before applying.

## Verification notes before you apply

Remind the reader what to confirm on the official source and what details should not be assumed.

warning: Add any risk note about salary ambiguity, shift timing, location changes, unofficial forms, or missing deadlines.
```

## Quality bar

- Aim for at least `180-300` words of original body content.
- Add reader-focused interpretation, not just reformatted employer text.
- Use the official source as the final authority for deadline, salary, and application flow.
- If a page cannot support strong editorial notes, leave it useful for visitors but do not treat it as index-worthy.
